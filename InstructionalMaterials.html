>Week 5: Monopolistic Competition/Oligopoly and Market Power

Understanding Market Structures and Consumer Behavior

Last week, we delved into the world of relatively non-existent market structures. This week, we jump headlong into the two most prevalent market structures in modern economies: monopolistic competition and oligopolies.

These two structures have similarities and can be difficult to distinguish. One firm may sell various brands of a product, creating the illusion of choice while still capturing all consumer spending.

As we discussed, monopolies are not very consumer-friendly. Anytime thereâ€™s less competition, consumers are disadvantaged. To counter this, governments enact laws to reduce monopoly power as industries consolidate.


Weekly Objectives

  • Identify key traits of monopolistic competition, including product differentiation and market entry conditions.
  • Compare and contrast oligopolistic market structures, focusing on firm behavior, pricing strategies, and barriers to entry.
  • Analyze factors that reduce market competition, such as mergers, acquisitions, and regulatory shifts.
  • Assess the role of government in controlling monopoly power, including antitrust laws and public policy tools.
